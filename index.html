Scenario-BasedAssessment

Activity 4 — Assessment Detective

Read each classroom snapshot. Identify the primary purpose of the assessment described: as, for, or of learning.

Scenario 1 of 5
Halfway through a Geography unit, the teacher pauses and shows a Mentimeter word cloud of student responses to "What factors affect where people choose to live?" She notices gaps and decides to reteach the environmental factors next lesson.
Scenario 2 of 5
At the end of a Civics and Citizenship unit, students complete an individual inquiry report. The teacher uses a rubric aligned to the Year 5 achievement standard to make a summative judgement about each student's attainment.
Scenario 3 of 5
Students review their KWL+ charts from the start of the unit, add to the 'L' and 'Q' columns, then write two goals for what they want to improve before the unit ends.
Scenario 4 of 5
The teacher circulates during a map annotation task, making anecdotal notes on which students can accurately identify human and physical features. She uses these notes to form flexible groups for the next activity.
Scenario 5 of 5
Before beginning a new HASS unit, students complete a self-assessment checklist rating their confidence with key vocabulary and concepts. They share their checklist with a peer who adds a comment.
